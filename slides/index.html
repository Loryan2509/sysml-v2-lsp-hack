The Opportunity

Models in Natural Language —
Made for LLMs

SysML v2 models describe entire systems — parts, actions, states, requirements — using structured natural language. That makes them a perfect use case for Large Language Models, which can read, reason over, and spot the issues that human reviewers leave behind.

📝
Natural Language Models
SysML v2 reads like structured English — part definitions, requirements, constraints. LLMs already understand this language natively.
💥
Human Reviewers Miss Issues
Large models with thousands of interconnected elements hide subtle flaws — missing failure modes, conflicting requirements, incomplete safety paths.
🤖
LLMs Can Spot What We Miss
Give an LLM the full model context and structured criteria, and it finds emergent issues, redundancies, and gaps that slip past expert review.

Key Learnings

What We Learnt
Along the Way

🎯 The Criteria is Key
Structured criteria are what turn an LLM from a chatbot into a systematic reviewer. Without them, you get vague opinions. With them, you get auditable, repeatable assessments that surface real issues every time.
💡 More Issues Than We Expected
We expected to find one or two issues per model. But with criteria guiding the AI, it consistently surfaced more valid findings than anticipated — subtle gaps in failure modes, unspoken assumptions, and emergent risks that were genuinely important.
🚀 Going Forward — Standards like ISO 15288
The same approach can extend beyond our 10 criteria. Standards like ISO 15288 could be encoded as additional criteria packs, letting the AI review models against formal engineering standards — not just best practices.
